## The capability problem is solved. The deployment problem is not.

In June 2026, Anthropic published data showing that AI systems can now autonomously complete
tasks that take skilled humans 12 hours. More than 80% of Anthropic's own production code is
written by Claude. Engineers are shipping 8x as much code per quarter as they were in 2024.

The frontier models are ready. The bottleneck has moved.

It has moved to **infrastructure, compliance, and regional fit** — the exact problems Glapagos
was built to solve.

---

## What the acceleration means for the Americas

Anthropic's analysis identifies three things that recursive self-improvement cannot speed up:

- **Regulation.** AI cannot make a jurisdiction adopt a new compliance framework faster than
  its institutions allow.
- **Trust.** AI cannot turn a regional dataset into a trusted production model without the
  data pipelines, governance, and sovereignty controls that local organizations require.
- **Context.** Generic global platforms are trained and optimized for the markets that built
  them. The regulatory, linguistic, and industry realities of North, Central, and South America
  are not their priority.

The Anthropic article puts it plainly: *"the felt pace of this future will still be set by the
bottlenecks."* For the Americas, those bottlenecks are not model capability. They are
infrastructure, jurisdiction, and access.

## The productivity gap is already opening

Anthropic's data shows that a 100-person organization using AI can now do the work of a
10,000-person one — but only if it has the infrastructure to deploy AI across its operations.

Without that infrastructure, the productivity gains stay concentrated in organizations that
already have the engineering resources to build and maintain it themselves. For most
organizations across the Americas — enterprises, government agencies, regional industries —
that is not a realistic option.

This is the gap Glapagos closes.
